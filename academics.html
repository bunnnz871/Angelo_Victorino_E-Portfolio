Artifact 01

Prelim Term Reflection

Foundations

The screenshots from my Prelim term show a learning period that was both encouraging and humbling. In the beginning, I performed very well in several branching activities. I got perfect scores in the if statement, if...else, and if...else if...else activities, which showed me that I was able to follow logical conditions and understand how a program makes decisions. These activities gave me confidence because they proved that I was starting to think in a more systematic way when solving programming problems.

At the same time, the Prelim term also exposed the parts of programming that were more difficult for me. My score in the switch statement activity was only 10/50, and my score in the while loop activity was 30/50. I also had mixed results in some assessments, such as Quiz 2 with 11/55 and the Prelim Examination with 44/95. These results reminded me that understanding a topic during practice is different from mastering it under assessment pressure. They taught me that I needed more repetition, better review habits, and more careful tracing of program flow.

Even with those struggles, the Prelim term still contains some of my strongest evidence of growth. I earned high or perfect results in Do-while Loop, For Loop, Prelim Quiz 1, Hands-On Seatwork Arrays, and Machine Problem on MD Arrays. Those successes showed me that when I practiced enough, I could understand both repetition and array-based logic. I began to see that programming is not learned in one moment of understanding. It is built through repeated trial, mistakes, and correction.

Overall, the Prelim term built my foundation in branching, loops, and arrays while also teaching me an important lesson about consistency. The screenshots clearly show both strengths and weak points, and I think that makes them meaningful artifacts. They represent the stage where I first learned that programming requires patience, attention to detail, and the willingness to keep improving even when the results are not yet ideal.

Artifact 02

Midterm Reflection

Structure

The Midterm term shows a more stable and mature part of my programming journey. Based on the screenshots, this was the period where I worked on functions and methods, including methods with no parameters, methods with return values, and methods with parameters. I received perfect scores in all four of these activities, which tells me that I was already becoming more comfortable with organizing code into reusable parts instead of writing everything in one block.

What I learned most during this term was that programming becomes clearer when logic is divided into methods with specific purposes. Working with parameters and return values helped me understand how information moves through a program. It also trained me to think more carefully about the relationship between input, process, and output. Compared with the Prelim term, I felt that my code was becoming more organized and that I was gaining more control over how I solved problems.

However, the Midterm screenshots also show that I still had areas that needed improvement. My Machine Problem - Exceptions score was 13/20, which suggests that I was still learning how to handle errors and unexpected cases properly. Even though I did well in the method-based activities and scored 21/25 in Midterm Quiz 1, the exception task reminded me that clean logic is not enough. A program also needs to respond correctly when something goes wrong.

Looking back, the Midterm term was the stage where I became more independent and more structured as a learner. The evidence from this period shows stronger performance and better control of programming concepts. It taught me that good programming is not only about getting a correct answer, but also about writing solutions that are clear, modular, and ready to handle real situations.

Artifact 03

Final Term Reflection

Integration

The Final term reflects a shift from basic control flow and method construction into more advanced problem-solving using arrays and data structures. The screenshots show activities such as Functions/Methods with 1D Arrays, Machine Problem - Stacks & Queues, and Machine Problem on Stacks, Queues & Sorting. These topics required a deeper level of thinking because they involved organizing data, controlling order, and solving more layered programming tasks.

My results during this period show both growth and continued challenge. I scored 40/50 in the 1D array activity, which tells me that I understood the topic but still had room to improve in accuracy or implementation. I earned 40/40 in the Stacks & Queues machine problem, which was one of my strongest performances and a sign that I had become more confident in handling structured data. In the Stacks, Queues & Sorting machine problem, I scored 30/40, which shows progress but also reminds me that more complex algorithms still demanded careful study.

What I appreciate most about the Final term is that it tested not only what I knew, but how well I could combine different concepts into one solution. Arrays, stacks, queues, and sorting all require discipline in logic and attention to sequence. They also require more planning before coding. Through these activities, I learned that advanced programming topics are manageable when I approach them step by step and remain patient with debugging.

Overall, the Final term made me realize how far I had come since the start of the course. I moved from basic decision statements to topics that involve more abstract structures and more deliberate design. The evidence from these screenshots shows that I am still learning, but it also shows that I am already capable of solving more difficult programming problems. This term gave me confidence that I can continue improving in future programming subjects.
